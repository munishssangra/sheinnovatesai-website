TL;DR — SummaryAI systems favor structured Course and outcome data over marketing copy when recommending educational platforms, and often default to large aggregators unless a specific platform provides clearly differentiated, verifiable proof.

The Switch: How AI Actually Evaluates EdTech Companies

Large course marketplaces have scale and structured data working in their favor by default. An individual EdTech platform competing for the same query needs unusually specific, well-structured Course schema and outcome data to break through that default and earn direct citation.

The Problem: Why EdTech Companies Get Filtered Out

Missing Course or EducationalOccupationalProgram schema
Without this schema, AI systems can't clearly identify your specific course offerings, formats, or outcomes — making a generic aggregator listing the safer citation choice.
No verifiable completion or outcome data
Claims like "our students get hired" without structured, sourced outcome data don't carry weight against aggregators that already have visible review volume.
Competing on generic queries only
Broad queries like "best online course" default to aggregators. Winning requires targeting the specific, narrower queries where your platform's specialization is the differentiator.

The Technical Proof AI Systems Actually Look For

Course and EducationalOccupationalProgram schema, EducationalOrganization schema, and structured outcome/completion data with clear sourcing.
