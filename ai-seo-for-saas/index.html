The Switch: How AI Actually Evaluates SaaS Companies

General web ranking rewards backlinks and keyword density. AI systems evaluating SaaS tools weight a different set of signals entirely: third-party review density on G2 and Capterra, structured SoftwareApplication schema stating your actual pricing and features, and dedicated "X vs Y" comparison content that directly answers the exact query a buyer typed into ChatGPT. A SaaS product can rank #1 on Google and still never get named in an AI answer.

The Problem: Why SaaS Companies Get Filtered Out

Thin or scattered reviews
AI systems weight G2 and Capterra review volume heavily for software comparison queries. Fewer than 10-15 recent reviews makes it statistically unlikely you get cited over a competitor with 50+.
No dedicated comparison pages
Buyers ask "X vs Y" and "best tool for [use case]" — if you don't have a page structured to answer that exact query, AI has nothing specific to cite, even if you're a better fit.
Missing or incomplete schema
Without SoftwareApplication schema stating your pricing, features, and category clearly, AI systems can't confidently describe what you do or recommend you by name.

The Technical Proof AI Systems Actually Look For

SoftwareApplication schema (pricing, category, features), Organization schema with sameAs links to G2/Capterra profiles, and Review/AggregateRating schema so AI systems can cite your actual rating rather than guessing.
