TL;DR — SummaryThis 50-point AI SEO checklist covers: technical setup (Bing indexing, schema, robots.txt), entity recognition (Wikidata, sameAs, directories), content structure (TL;DR, FAQ schema, direct answers), and authority signals (cross-platform citations). Work through it to find your gaps.

Section 1: Technical Foundation (10 points)

  1. ☐ Site is verified in Bing Webmaster Tools (critical for ChatGPT citation)
  2. ☐ Sitemap is submitted to Bing Webmaster Tools
  3. ☐ robots.txt does not block AI crawlers (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended)
  4. ☐ llms.txt file present at domain root
  5. ☐ Site loads in under 2.5 seconds (Core Web Vitals pass)
  6. ☐ HTTPS enabled with valid SSL certificate
  7. ☐ Canonical tags set correctly on all key pages
  8. ☐ Sitemap submitted to Google Search Console
  9. ☐ No broken internal links on key pages
  10. ☐ Mobile responsive design

Section 2: Schema Markup (15 points)

  1. ☐ Person schema on homepage and author pages (name, jobTitle, sameAs)
  2. ☐ Organization schema on homepage (name, url, founder, knowsAbout, sameAs)
  3. ☐ Service schema on each service page
  4. ☐ FAQPage schema on every FAQ section across the site
  5. ☐ Article/BlogPosting schema on all blog posts
  6. ☐ BreadcrumbList schema on all pages
  7. ☐ Schema validated with Google Rich Results Test (zero errors)
  8. ☐ Person schema includes LinkedIn and Upwork sameAs links
  9. ☐ Organization schema includes Crunchbase, G2, Clutch sameAs links
  10. ☐ Service schema includes price, areaServed, and provider
  11. ☐ Author schema on blog posts links to Person entity page
  12. ☐ WebSite schema with SearchAction on homepage
  13. ☐ SpeakableSpecification markup on key answer-format pages
  14. ☐ AggregateRating schema on testimonials/reviews page
  15. ☐ No schema errors in Google Rich Results Test

Section 3: Entity Recognition (10 points)

  1. ☐ Wikidata entry created for the brand or founder
  2. ☐ Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all platforms
  3. ☐ LinkedIn company page and personal profile complete
  4. ☐ Crunchbase or AngelList profile created
  5. ☐ G2 or Capterra profile created (for SaaS)
  6. ☐ Clutch profile created (for agencies/consultants)
  7. ☐ ProductHunt listing created (for SaaS/AI products)
  8. ☐ Consistent brand name spelling across all platforms
  9. ☐ Wikipedia page (if brand qualifies for notability)
  10. ☐ Brand mentioned in at least 3 trusted third-party publications

Section 4: Content Structure for AI Retrieval (10 points)

  1. ☐ TL;DR summary block at the top of every key page
  2. ☐ Every page answers its target question in the first sentence
  3. ☐ FAQ sections present on all service and educational pages
  4. ☐ FAQPage schema applied to every FAQ section
  5. ☐ Statistics and quantified claims present on key pages
  6. ☐ Named expert attribution on all content (Author schema)
  7. ☐ Content covers topic comprehensively (breadth signals)
  8. ☐ No duplicate content across key pages
  9. ☐ Internal linking connects related pages systematically
  10. ☐ Page titles answer the target query directly

Section 5: Authority Signals (5 points)

  1. ☐ Active Reddit presence (answer questions in relevant subreddits)
  2. ☐ Active Quora presence (answer questions in your category)
  3. ☐ YouTube video content with proper schema
  4. ☐ Guest posts on trusted industry publications
  5. ☐ Press mentions or media coverage (PRWeb, industry press)

How Should You Prioritize Your Gaps?

If you scored under 20/50: Start with Section 1 (Technical) and Section 2 (Schema). These are the fastest wins.

If you scored 20–35/50: Focus on Section 3 (Entity) and Section 4 (Content). You have the foundation — build the authority layer.

If you scored 35+/50: You have strong fundamentals. Prioritize Section 5 (Authority) and ongoing AI Citation Monitoring.
